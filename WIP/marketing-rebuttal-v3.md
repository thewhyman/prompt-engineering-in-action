### Superpower 2: The Token Multiplier — A Linting Layer for Human Intent

**Every bad prompt costs you twice — once for the wrong answer, once for the correction.**

In our internal testing across 200+ sessions, users reached their target answer in 1-2 exchanges instead of 4-5. Each exchange costs tokens. On API-powered tools (Cursor, Windsurf, Cline), that's $0.003-0.015 per exchange. Over a month: you're paying 3-5x more than you need to.

Co-Dialectic sharpens every prompt in real-time. Before the AI answers your vague question with a vague response, it flags specificity.

**"If the AI can tell my prompt is vague, why can't it just answer correctly?"** Because it can tell the prompt is vague — it just can't guess which direction you meant. The score measures specificity: did you give enough constraints for one right answer, or are there ten valid interpretations? That's a question only you can answer. The sharpening closes that gap.

**"Won't this get annoying?"** In ⚡ Flow mode (auto-detected in IDEs and coding contexts), it never stops you. It executes immediately and appends the sharpening tip at the end. In 🛑 Refine mode, it only pauses on genuinely ambiguous prompts. **It gets out of your way as you get better.**

**The compounding effect:** you're not just saving tokens today. You're learning to prompt better. After 10 days, your prompt quality visibly improves — you can see the scores in real-time. The sharpening becomes unnecessary because YOU became the expert.

You save tokens twice: once from immediate sharpening, and permanently from learned skill.

> **Dev hook:** "I reduced my chat-to-result ratio from 4:1 to 1:1. Co-Dialectic is a linting layer for human intent."
> **General hook:** "What if your AI refused to waste your money on a bad answer?"

### Superpower 3: A Smoke Detector for Your Conversation

**Every other AI tool has a dirty secret: it's silently forgetting your conversation.**

When your chat gets long, AI tools auto-compress. They silently summarize earlier messages, dropping details they decided weren't important. You don't know it happened. The AI doesn't tell you. It just starts giving subtly worse answers.

Co-Dialectic estimates your context health based on conversation density and alerts you before degradation begins. It can't see inside the model's memory — no tool can. But it's calibrated across hundreds of sessions to catch the danger zone before you hit it. **Think of it as a smoke detector for your conversation** — it doesn't measure the fire, but it saves your work.

```
🟢 — context fresh. Full accuracy.
🟡 — context working. Still accurate.
🔴 — context critical. Handoff initiated.
```

When context hits 🔴, Co-Dialectic writes your conversation's DNA — every requirement, correction, and preference — into a portable handoff block. You paste it into a fresh conversation and pick up exactly where you left off. One extra step. Zero lost context.

**The handoff is model-agnostic.** You can teleport your AI's learned preferences from Claude to GPT-4o to a local Llama instance. Your AI history becomes a portable asset — not locked into any platform.

**"How is this different from ChatGPT Memory?"** ChatGPT Memory remembers facts about you. Co-Dialectic sharpens how you think. Memory stores "user prefers Python." Co-Dialectic tracks whether your prompts are specific enough to get the right answer on the first try — and teaches you to make them better.

> **Hook:** "Your AI is silently forgetting your conversation. Mine tells me when it's about to forget — and saves everything important before it does."
> **Dev hook:** "LLMs silently truncate. Co-Dialectic implements a state-persistence layer for long-running engineering sessions."

## Progressive Guidance (The Architecture of Skill)

Co-Dialectic doesn't hand you a manual. It teaches itself to you through progressive disclosure — the same UX pattern used by the best games and professional tools.

**The system detects your skill level** from prompt quality trends, command usage, and conversation depth. Then it meets you where you are:

| Skill Level | What You See | What You're Learning |
|---|---|---|
| **New** (first ~5 interactions) | `💡 "cod help" · "cod details" · "Be Jony Ive"` | Basic commands exist. Personas can be set. |
| **Intermediate** (used a few commands) | `💡 "cod flow" · "cod refine" · "Channel Linus for this bug"` | Pacing modes. Specific persona invocation. |
| **Advanced** (prompt quality consistently ✅) | `💡 "Ive + Jobs for this landing page" · "cod teach"` | Multi-persona fusion. Self-review. Teaching mode. |

Every hint is contextual. Every feature is discovered at the moment you're ready for it. You never feel overwhelmed because the system only shows you the next level — never three levels ahead.

This is dogfooding at its purest: the architecture of Co-Dialectic IS the architecture of skill acquisition itself.

## Tone Selector (Planned — v2.2)

**Problem:** Most AI tools default to cheerleader mode. "Great question!" "You're doing amazing!" This creates a social dilemma bubble where the user never gets real feedback.

**Solution:** Let users set their preferred feedback tone:

| Command | Tone | Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| `cod tone critical` | **Harshest critic** | No sugar-coating. Points out weak thinking directly. "This prompt is vague because..." |
| `cod tone grounded` | **Grounded guide** (default) | Balanced. Honest about gaps, constructive about fixes. |
| `cod tone cheerleader` | **Encourager** | Emphasizes progress. Best for new users building confidence. |

The tone affects how sharpening feedback is delivered, how persona switches are explained, and how milestone celebrations land. Same substance, different delivery.

## SKILL.md Compression (Planned — v2.2)

**Problem:** The full SKILL.md is ~16KB. Every token in the system prompt is paid on every request by every user.

**Solution:** Split into a terse core prompt (~4KB) + extended reference files loaded on demand:

```
SKILL.md (core)          — personas, status line, sharpening rules, context tracking
SKILL-extended.md        — detailed examples, philosophy, teaching protocols
SKILL-personalization.md — tone selector, gamification, advanced features
```

For Claude Code plugin users, the plugin system loads referenced files automatically. For manual paste users, the core prompt works standalone — extended features activate via `cod extend` which instructs the AI to fetch the extended file from the repository URL already in the metadata.

Token savings: ~60% reduction in per-request system prompt cost while keeping full functionality.
